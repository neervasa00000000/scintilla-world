Privacy Policy for SafeGuard Extension

Effective Date: December 20, 2025

This Privacy Policy outlines how the SafeGuard browser extension ("the Extension," "we," "us," or "our") collects, uses, maintains, and discloses information from users ("you" or "your") of the SafeGuard extension. SafeGuard is designed to enhance your security when interacting with Web3 applications and blockchain networks by providing risk assessments of transactions.

1. Data Minimization and Collection

The SafeGuard Extension operates with a strong commitment to data minimization. We only access and process the information strictly necessary to perform its stated function:

  • Public Blockchain Transaction Data: The Extension intercepts and analyzes transaction requests initiated by your browser to public blockchain networks. This data typically includes recipient addresses, transaction values, contract interaction data (e.g., function calls and parameters), and the associated blockchain network ID. This information is inherently public on the blockchain.
  • No Private Key Access: Crucially, SafeGuard does not access, collect, store, or transmit your private keys, seed phrases, or any other sensitive wallet credentials. Your wallet security remains entirely in your control.
  • No Personal Identifiable Information (PII): We do not intentionally collect any personal identifiable information (PII) such as your name, email address, IP address, or browsing history outside of the immediate transaction context.
  • Local Processing: All transaction analysis and risk assessments are performed locally within your browser environment. No transaction data or related analysis results are sent to our servers or any third-party servers for processing or storage.

2. How We Use Information

The information accessed by SafeGuard is used solely for the following purposes:

  • To analyze and simulate blockchain transactions in real-time within your browser.
  • To identify potential risks, malicious patterns, or suspicious activities related to proposed transactions.
  • To display clear, actionable warnings and summaries to you before you approve a transaction.
  • To block known malicious transactions based on publicly available security databases (e.g., blocklists).

We do not use any collected information for advertising, marketing, or any purpose unrelated to the core security function of the Extension.

3. Data Storage and Security

As analysis is performed locally, the Extension does not store transaction data persistently on your device beyond the immediate context required for a pending transaction. Any temporary data used for analysis is purged once a transaction is approved, rejected, or expires.

We implement industry-standard security measures to protect the integrity of the Extension itself and the data it processes locally. However, please be aware that no software or internet transmission can be guaranteed to be 100% secure.
