What to Look For in Moringa Reviews

Not all moringa reviews are equal. When reading moringa reviews in Australia, look for these quality indicators:

Color

High-quality moringa is vivid green. If it's brownish or yellowish, it was over-dried or stored poorly. Shade-dried moringa retains its green color and nutrient content.

Taste

Clean, slightly grassy, earthy. Musty, stale, or bitter tastes indicate old stock or poor processing. Fresh moringa should not taste rancid.

Lab Testing

The best brands provide independent lab certificates for heavy metals, pesticides, and microbial contamination. If a brand doesn't mention testing, assume it wasn't done.

Origin

Australian-grown moringa is fresher (shorter supply chain) and subject to Australian food safety standards. Imported moringa may spend months in transit and storage.
