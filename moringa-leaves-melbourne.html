Fresh vs Dried Moringa Leaves in Melbourne

Fresh moringa leaves are extremely hard to find in Melbourne because the tree doesn't grow outdoors here (too cold in winter). Here's the practical breakdown:

  • Fresh leaves: Available only seasonally at select Indian grocers. Must be used within 2-3 days. Best for cooking (stir-fries, dals, teas).
  • Dried leaves: Widely available. Shelf-stable for 6-12 months. Use in teas, soups, and tempering. Lower vitamin C than fresh but retains most minerals.
  • Powder (shade-dried): Most convenient and concentrated. 1 teaspoon of powder = roughly 1 cup of fresh leaves in nutrient content. Easiest to incorporate into daily routine.

Can You Grow Moringa in Melbourne?

Melbourne's climate is too cold for outdoor moringa trees. Winter frost will kill them. However, you can grow moringa in large pots and bring them indoors during winter (June-August). The tree will drop leaves in cold weather but regrow in spring.
